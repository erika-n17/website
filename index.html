About Me

Hi! My name is Erika Narimatsu, and I'm a 2nd year mechanical engineering student at the University of Toronto. I love the hands-on aspect of mechanical engineering, and the pride in seeing something I designed come to life. In my free time, I love to bake, listen to music, and play video games.

My Inspirations

When I was in middle school, I had the opportunity to see ASIMO in person at the Miraikan in Tokyo, and watched the robot perform backflips and score soccer balls. I was amazed at how it was able to interact with audience members and behave like a human would. Learning about robotic systems like ASIMO, and my own experiences with FIRST Robotics inspired me to pursue engineering in post-secondary education.

My Aspirations

Robots and mechatronics create opportunities to enhance human life and allow us to challenge the boundaries of our own capability; robots like ASIMO and Pepper create new possibilities and care, therapy and rehabilitation, while Da Vinci's Intuitive allows us to increase surpass the limits of human precision and accuracy in surgery. In third year, I hope to specialize in mechatronics (and solid mechanics or bioengineering) to develop the skills and knowledge needed to create robotic systems. By contributing to the innovation of robotic systems, I hope to one day be part of something that helps change someone's life for the better.

Projects

Extracurriculars

Through course projects, as well as cocurricular engineering projects, I have developed technical proficiency in areas such as mechanical design, CAD, and FEA. Furthermore, these projects have helped me improve my skills in teamwork, communication, and organization.

Backplane Enclosure

May 2021 - Sept 2021
Robotics for Space Exploration

To protect the rover's circuit boards from sand and debris while providing easy access, I collaborated with the electrical team to create a backplane enclosure. The circuit boards slide into the enclosure to connect to a central circuit panel at the back of the enclosure, which eliminates messy wiring. This project helped me to effectively collaborate with individuals of differing technical backgrounds, and further understand the integration of different engineering disciplines in large scale projects.

ANSYS Workshop

Jul 2021
Robotics for Space Exploration

I curated a summer workshop to teach the basics of finite element analysis, and the use of ANSYS, to members of the design team. ANSYS was used to simulate a tensile test on dog-bone specimens of varying geometry; this enabled students to observe stress concentration and estimate yield stress on familiar models.

Rocker Bogie

Sep 2020 - Apr 2021
Robotics for Space Exploration

In the 2020-2021 season for RSX, I was responsible for the redesign of the rocker bogie drive system, which lowered the center of gravity and increased durability. Although we were unable to build the finalized design, the online school year presented me with an opportunity to further improve my verbal communication and listening skills.

Battery Bay Design

Sep 2019 - Apr 2020
Robotics for Space Exploration

In first year, I created the battery and circuit panel mount for RSX’s 2019-2020 Mars Rover, which is made of 3D printed PLA, and laser cut acrylic. In this project, I was required to design a system that was compact, robust, and allowed for quick battery exchanges during competitions. In this project, I learned how to effectively communicate with team members of diverse technical backgrounds to develop a design which met everyone’s needs.

Gearbox

Dec 2020
MIE243: Mechanical Engineering Design

In MIE243, our 2nd year mechanical engineering design course, we designed and 3D printed gearboxes in teams of 4 to apply the knowledge we had acquired in class; I was responsible for the design of the housings, as well as the assembly of the gearbox CAD. This gearbox has a 16:1 reduction and collinear input-output. In this project, I learned how to apply the correct tolerances to a design, and optimize design for ease of manufacturing and assembly.
